When you're in the middle of having a serious problem, it feels absolutely impossible to scale it.

Even if you break it down into smaller chunks and actionable things, you get so overwhelmed by the amount of stuff that still needs to be made.

One of the common advice given then is to look back at the past problems and notice that they felt the same way, and yet still, they weren't that hard in the end.
I just wish I didn't romanticize the process of being overwhelmed by difficult things as much as I do.

You can be absolutely crushed by a thing in the moment and feeling like a martyr for putting even more stuff on your shoulders.

I'm still mentally in the Tumblr Sad Girl era, thanks for ruining my life forever.
