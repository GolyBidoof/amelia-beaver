I exist strongly in two states: being physically fit, but mentally exhausted, or being physically exhausted, but mentally fit.

That's why I love physical activity, it keeps me sharp mentally. The bigger the slump, the more times you spin around your activity watch circles, right?

I had very uncomfortable shoes for a while, and that's when I realized how much I depend on my legs to just be able to function normally.
You grit your teeth, put another band-aid, a gel one, and hope that you'll still be able to keep walking for the next few hours.

And then you get back to the hostel, 40k steps in, rip your band-aids off...

What if "being kind to yourself" mentally is "being not kind to your own body", and vice-versa?

Ah, the healthy coping mechanisms.
